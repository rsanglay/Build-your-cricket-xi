Frequently asked questions

What is The Perfect XI?

A free cricket draft game: build an all-time XI one player at a time, then simulate a full campaign to see if it could go unbeaten. A cricket take on the viral football game 38-0 and the NBA game 82-0.

How do I play?

Pick a mode, then Classic (ratings shown) or Expert (ratings hidden). Each round a wheel spins a random Nation × Era — draft one player from that group and they slot into the best open position. Three rerolls per game. Fill all 11, then the simulator plays out your campaign match by match and shows your record, the scorelines, and the awards.

What are the two modes?

World Cup (ODI) — a one-day XI: top a 10-team group, win the semi-final and final, all 11 matches, for the 11–0. World Test Championship — a Test XI through a 6-series home-and-away cycle, finish top two, win the one-off final, go unbeaten for the mace.

How does multiplayer work?

After drafting, tap “Share your XI” to copy a short code and send it to a friend. They open the game, hit “Play a tournament”, paste your code (and others'), and run a World Cup or WTC tournament between the real teams. Empty seats are filled with AI sides. No accounts, no server — the code is the whole transport, and the same codes always produce the same tournament.

How is a result and margin decided?

A transparent ratings model decides each match, then a realistic scoreline is generated to match it: a win batting first is by runs, a successful chase is by wickets, a Test can be won by an innings or end in a draw — exactly as in real cricket.

Are the ratings official? Does my data leave the page?

No on both counts. Player ratings are deliberately subjective, for-fun estimates — not official rankings. Everything runs in your browser; nothing is uploaded or stored on a server. Share codes are only shared by you.
